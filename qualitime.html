QualyTime your hobby friend

Role & Scope

  • Project type: Web-responsive app
  • Duration: 7 months
  • Role: UX Design from Concept to MVP and Prototype
  • Project context: Careerfoundry coursework Project
  • Team size: 3 people - me, a Mentor, and a Tutor

Project Details

  • Audience: Hobbyists, Millenials
  • Methods: Ideation, User Research, IA, User Testing, UI, Prototyping
  • Tools: Figma, Miro

Careerfoundry "Ask-an-Expert" Business Requierments

  • Browse and choose an expert
  • Chat with an expert
  • Arrange a call with an expert
  • Pay for expert's time

Introduction

QualyTime empowers hobbyists at all levels with the tools and guidance they need to learn, advance, and make the most of their time, offering an array of inspirational materials and expert advice.

Research & Discovery

Interview Research Goals

  • How do people choose, prepare, start, and progress in an activity;
  • What supports people best in their hobbies- materials, best practices etc;
  • When do people feel trust towards information sources in unfamiliar fields;

Research informed hypothesis

Hobbyists need a video-based learning platform, whose content creators serve as teachers and advisors, thereby fostering skill advancement and time optimization.

Research Insights

The 5 one hour interviews with people from the target audience uncovered that:

  1. People feel intimidated by the word “expert”. Calling the app-experts Teachers would make the hobbyists feel more comfortable reaching out.
  2. Reaching out to an online stranger is hard. People would like to know more about the teachers personality and life- provide a Teachers Profile with personal statement, credentials and photos.
  3. People prefer to learn with video tutorials, when possible. Finding the “right” learning materials is so frustrating, that people would often give up early on. Providing expert-proofed video-tutorials created by the QualyTime teachers might help with that problem.

Creating User Personas

Primary

A die-hard-hobbyist- for them, their creations are an important part of their identity. They are skilled and know what they need to learn or improve. For them, finding the “right” teacher is a main priority.

Secondary

A casual hobby-enthusiast: they enjoy spending their limited free time doing something creative or trying out new things. For them the access to curated learning materials and good advice is most important.

Creating Journey Maps

The journey maps provided detailed insights into what hobbyists might aim to accomplish with QualyTime, as well as the features that could facilitate these tasks more effortlessly.

Research Conclusion

Based on the user research, user personas, and user journey maps, I concluded that the most useful MVP for hobbyists would provide:

  • A way to browse, sort and filter lessons;
  • A Lesson Area containing information on the project, its teacher, tutorials, and support materials;
  • A way to call and chat with a teacher.

Ideation

Process

To define the app's Information Architecture and identify its key features, I initially created User Flows. These subsequently evolved into a Sitemap, which was then refined and validated through an iterative process of card sorting.

Browsing

The landing page serves as the initial point for users to browse and search through tutorials and hobby categories.

Lesson Area

The contents of the Lesson Area are organized through a tab menu, including:

  1. Information on lesson details, such as content description, images, duration, difficulty, creator, and related videos
  2. Video tutorials and learning materials

Teacher's Profile

3. For the user's convenience, the third tab was transformed into a "Teacher's Profile" section. This section provides information on the teacher's creative path, personal work, and interests, offering users the option to book a call or initiate a chat.

Testing & Iterating

Usability Testing

The app's Information Architecture and Navigation patterns underwent validation through moderated Usability Tests and an A/B Test. However, these tests also uncovered usability issues within the app's UI.

Test Details

6 Moderated in-person and remote Usability Tests A/B Test with 20 participants, unmoderated, remote.

Test Objectives

  • Asses Navigation Efficiency: Evaluate the user's ability to navigate the app effortlessly, focusing on elements like search functionality, menus, browsing experience, and tab accessibility.
  • Ease-of-contact Evaluation: Examine the simplicity and intuitiveness of contacting a teacher through the app.
  • Ongoing Communications Accessibility: Assess how easily users can locate and access ongoing communications within the app.

UI Problem

When prompted to watch a video, testers frequently misclicked the image-carousel in the Lesson Area's first tab, leading to confusion and potential app abandonment. This issue also adversely affects the discoverability of the tab pattern.

Solution

A solution would be to implement a visually prominent tab menu and a distinctive image carousel, while also leveraging the principles of Material Design 3 to improve video discoverability.

High-fidelity Prototype

To provide an immersive experience for all individuals interested in the QualyTime project, I developed a high-fidelity prototype. To maximize the effectiveness and functionality of the product, I adhered to the Level A usability and Level AAA color contrast standards as outlined in the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines.
